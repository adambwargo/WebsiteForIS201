About Alaska

I grew up in Wasilla, Alaska — a town in the Mat-Su Valley, about 45 minutes north of Anchorage. Alaska isn't just a place I'm from; it shaped who I am. The outdoors, the seasons, the community — all of it stays with you.

Alaska is the largest state in the US by a wide margin. It's home to some of the most dramatic landscapes on Earth: towering mountain ranges, sprawling glaciers, dense boreal forests, and thousands of miles of untouched coastline.

Denali — 20,310 ft, the highest peak in North America
Things To Do in Alaska
  1. Explore the wilderness
    • Hiking in Denali National Park
    • Kayaking through Prince William Sound
    • Backpacking the Chugach Mountains
  2. Watch the Northern Lights
    • Best viewed September through March
    • Fairbanks offers some of the clearest views
    • Mat-Su Valley is also a great spot
  3. Go fishing
    • King salmon on the Kenai River
    • Halibut fishing out of Homer
    • Rainbow trout in countless backcountry lakes
  4. Winter sports
    • Dog mushing — including the famous Iditarod
    • Skiing at Alyeska Resort in Girdwood
    • Snowmobiling through the backcountry
